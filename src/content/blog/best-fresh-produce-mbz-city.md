## 1. Check the Aroma

Fresh fruits and vegetables should smell like what they are. Ripe mangoes emit a sweet, tropical fragrance at the stem end. Fresh herbs like basil and mint release their essential oils with a gentle rub. Even tomatoes and cucumbers have a subtle, earthy freshness when they are recently harvested.

If produce smells musty, fermented, or has no scent at all, it is likely past its prime or was harvested too early. At KA Fresh, we check aroma as part of our daily quality inspection because it is one of the most reliable indicators of freshness.

## 2. Examine Color and Texture

Vibrant, consistent color is a strong indicator of freshness. Look for leafy greens with deep, even coloring and no yellowing or brown spots. Tomatoes should have a rich red or orange hue depending on the variety, without green shoulders unless you specifically want underripe ones for cooking.

Texture matters too. Carrots and cucumbers should feel firm and crisp, not rubbery or limp. Apples and pears should be free of soft spots or bruising. Our team removes any items that do not meet these standards before they ever reach our shelves.

## 3. Look at the Stems and Leaves

The condition of stems and leaves tells you a lot about how recently produce was harvested. Fresh coriander and parsley should have perky, upright stems and no wilting. Leafy greens like spinach and lettuce should have turgid leaves that snap rather than bend.

For fruits, check the stem end. A fresh stem that is still slightly green indicates recent harvest. Dry, brown, or missing stems often mean the fruit has been in storage or transit for an extended period.

## 4. Consider Weight and Density

Quality produce typically feels heavy for its size, indicating high water content and density. A juicy watermelon, ripe pomegranate, or firm eggplant should feel substantial in your hand. Lightweight produce may be dehydrated or past its peak.

This is particularly important for citrus fruits, melons, and root vegetables. Fresh produce is not just about taste, though that is certainly important. Fresher fruits and vegetables retain more vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. They also last longer in your refrigerator, reducing food waste and saving you money.
